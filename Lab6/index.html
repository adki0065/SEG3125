Pets can be spayed or neutered once they reach 6 months of age. Recommended for all cats, dogs and rabbits. Spaying a pet entails surgical removal of the female reproductive tract, whereas neutering a male entails surgical removal of the testicles. Spaying a female pet avoids the inconvenience of heat cycles, eliminates life-threatening infections of the uterus, and decreases the risk of breast cancer. Neutering a male pet decreases the incidence of tumors of the anus, rectum, testicles, & prostate. Surgery may also decrease the tendency to stray or roam. Neutering a domestic rabbit can also decrease aggressive tendencies, biting, and thumping the hind feet.

Veterenarians

Dr. Allison Finlay

Born and raised in the Leaside neighbourhood of Toronto. She began her career in the same area after graduating from the Ontario Veterinary College in 2007, and moved to Ottawa with her husband in fall of the same year. Dr. Finlay has been practicing in Ottawa ever since. Her professional interests include wellness and geriatric medicine as well as oncology (the treatment of cancer), and especially cherishes the bonds that she is able to form with both her patients and their owners


Dr. Keith Johnson

Dr. Johnson graduated from the Atlantic Veterinary College in 1999. Upon completion of his studies, he returned to his native Ontario to join Carling Animal Hospital. Dr Johnson is a co-owner of Carling Animal Hospital along with Dr Saye Clement. His professional interests include all aspects of veterinary medicine, Laser surgery, and dentistry in companion animals including dogs, cats, rabbits and pocket pets.


Dr. Melanie Ellison

A native of Bermuda who adopted Canada as her home following graduation from the Ontario Veterinary College in 1987, Dr. Ellison holds a particular interest in veterinary dentistry and medicine. Having gained 10 years experience in the Toronto area, Dr. Ellison and her family relocated to Ottawa in 1998, when she joined our practice. An avid sailor and gardener, Dr. Ellison enjoys spending time with her husband, daughter and son at their rural home just west of the City.
